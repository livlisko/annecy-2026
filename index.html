Where we'll actually go

Town-by-town swim guide.

The lake is small. Each town has its own personality — sandy beach, pontoon, market morning, long dinner. Pick a base for the day, swim there, eat there, leave when it stops being fun.

Home base Plage municipale Voie Verte

Sévrier

West-shore village, 10 min from old Annecy. The default swim — walk down, get in, walk back. No plan required. Bike path runs straight outside the door.

Vibe
Quiet, residential, lake-adjacent. Where we sleep and reset between bigger days.
Water
Plage municipale de Sévrier — straightforward swim, paddle/SUP rentals around the marina.
Food
Le Poisson Rouge for a real lakeside dinner. Bakery + Casino supermarket for everyday.
When
This is where we default to a swim without making it a plan. Mornings, recovery days, after-dinner walks.
Sandy beach Pavillon Bleu

Saint-Jorioz

The only real sandy beach on the lake. Lawn, shade, volleyball, pétanque, diving board. Big group day.

Best for
Lazy beach day with everything in one place.
Water
Wide swim area, supervised in July/August, easy entry, diving board.
Food
Thursday morning marché. Snack stands at the beach.
When
Hot afternoon. Budget-friendly day. When someone wants real sand.
Storybook village Bike-and-lunch

Duingt

The bend in the lake. Tiny village, the famous château-on-the-water, lunch terraces with the view. One of the prettiest stops on the loop.

Best for
Bike to lunch and a swim. The "wait, this is real" photo moment.
Water
Small public swim entries near the village — scout a spot, get in, dry off.
Food
Le Bon Wagon for terrace lunch with the lake. Pump track nearby for the brothers.
When
Mid-loop ride break. Long lunch day.
Quiet swim Bout-du-Lac

Doussard / Bout-du-Lac

South tip of the lake. Nature reserve, reedy edges, calmer water, fewer people. The "we don't want a scene today" beach.

Best for
Recovery day swim. Wildlife / nature trail. Slow afternoon.
Water
Free beach (Douss'plage area). Quiet entries along the south shore.
Food
Bring a picnic from the Sévrier or Saint-Jorioz market. Limited options on site.
When
Hot Sunday. After a big bike day. When the north end feels too busy.
Diving board · slide Pretty bay

Talloires

Big lake-day energy. Trees, lawn, sand, paddling pool, diving board, slide, a nautical center. Postcard bay, view across to Duingt's château.

Best for
The "do everything in the water" day with the brothers.
Water
Nautical center, diving board, slide, paddling pool. SUP/kayak rentals nearby.
Food
Several lakeside restaurants. Reserve dinner ahead in August.
When
The bigger lake day. Pair with east-shore drive or boat shuttle.
Pontoon dining Cascade nearby

Angon

Just south of Talloires. Beach with a small stream running through it. Gorgeous water, view to Duingt château, and L'Aquarama on a pontoon over the water.

Best for
Long lunch on the water, then swim, then dry off in the grass.
Water
Beach with stream entry, pontoon swimming. Cascade d'Angon is a short walk inland (waterfall, ground-level).
Food
L'Aquarama on the floating pontoon — the most "are we in a movie" lunch on the lake.
When
Golden hour swim. Slow lunch. Pretty afternoon photos without effort.
Pontoons · diving boards Château view

Menthon-Saint-Bernard

East-shore beach with a promenade, fixed and floating pontoons, diving boards, snack/restaurant on site. Storybook château on the hill above town.

Best for
Pontoon-and-dive afternoon. Pretty evening stop.
Water
Multiple pontoons + diving boards. Easy swim out, lots to climb on.
Food
Beach snack/restaurant. Town has a few proper dinner spots.
When
Hot afternoon when we want pontoons. Evening drive on the way back from Talloires.
Free / public View back to Annecy

Veyrier-du-Lac · La Brune

Public Pavillon Bleu beach on the east side, just south of Annecy. Clean swim, sunny grass and sand, volleyball, pétanque, diving board. The view back across the lake is the best part.

Best for
Clean east-side swim with the prettiest view back to old Annecy.
Water
Free, supervised in summer, diving board, gentle entry.
Food
Town restaurants up the hill. Build a picnic before you go.
When
Half-day east side stop. Sunset light is unreal here.
Old town · canals Markets · gelato

Annecy & Vieux Annecy

The anchor city. Canals, stone streets, markets, gelato, lakefront walks, restaurants. Marquisats and Albigny give you swim spots within town walking distance.

Best for
Wandering, market mornings, long dinners, the festival evenings.
Water
Plage des Marquisats (free, in town) or Albigny / Plage de l'Impérial just up the lakefront.
Food
Tuesday/Friday/Sunday market in the old town. Reserve dinners a day ahead in August.
When
Arrival day, market morning, evening art walk, anywhere we need a "no logistics" day.
Scout / watch only Nature reserve

Roc de Chère

Wooded reserve on the east shore between Menthon and Talloires. Clear water, hidden swim entries, locals jumping from rocks — not our move (group is height-shy). Worth a walk to look.

Best for
Curiosity walk, water-watch, photos. Not cliff jumping.
Water
Quiet entries from low rocks if you scout carefully. Skip anything exposed.
Food
None on site. Pair with Talloires lunch.
When
Cool half-day walk. Pair with a Talloires/Angon swim afterward.

Where things are

Lac d'Annecy at a glance.

Annecy at the north. Sévrier is our home base on the west shore. Sandy beaches and the loop ride hug the west; pontoons, diving boards and the prettiest bays are on the east. Buses link both shores from town.

Home base

Sévrier

Practical west-shore village, 10 minutes from old Annecy by bus or bike path. Easy lake access, no logistics overhead, and the Voie Verte starts right outside.

What this means for the day: walk to the water, ride to old town, swim on the way back. Repeat.

Lake bus lines

Line 50A — west shore Annecy ↔ Sévrier ↔ Saint-Jorioz ↔ Duingt ↔ Doussard.
Line 60 — east shore Annecy ↔ Veyrier-du-Lac ↔ Menthon-Saint-Bernard ↔ Talloires ↔ Angon.
Boat shuttle Compagnie des Bateaux runs a summer shuttle linking towns around the lake — handy when nobody wants to drive.

Easy Water Daily default

Lake swims & beach afternoons

Default activity: walk or bike to the nearest beach, swim, snack, repeat. Lac d'Annecy is unusually clear and very swim-friendly all summer. See the town-by-town swim guide above for which beach fits which day.

French: plage = beach. May be grass, pebbles, or sand — only Saint-Jorioz is true ocean-style sand.
Official beach list Saint-Jorioz beach
Water Easy Brothers approved

Pontoons, diving boards & floating docks

The "we want to jump off something" day. Menthon-Saint-Bernard has fixed and floating pontoons plus diving boards. Talloires has a nautical center with diving board and slide. Saint-Jorioz and Veyrier (La Brune) both have public diving boards. Angon's L'Aquarama puts you on a floating restaurant pontoon.

Heads up: some private pontoons exist on the lake, but only the ones at public beaches / nautical centers are open to all. Don't assume — scout first, ask a local.

Water Easy

SUP, kayak, pedal boat

Rentals at Sévrier, Saint-Jorioz, Talloires, and the old-town lakefront. Calm summer mornings are the move — afternoon thermals can pick up wind on the petit lac. SUP across the bay is a slow, easy win.

French: paddle = SUP. pédalo = pedal boat.

Easy → medium Bike

The lakeside ride (with swim stops)

The classic move: ride south on the Voie Verte from Sévrier toward Doussard, swim wherever it looks good, eat in Duingt, ride back. The full 40 km loop is here if anyone wants it. E-bikes very welcome.

French: voie verte = greenway, the bike/pedestrian path separated from cars.

Medium Mountain Height-safe

Mountain views from the ground

The big-picture mountain experience without ridges, chains, or exposure. Drive up to the Col de la Forclaz for the cinematic view down the petit lac and watch paragliders launch from the lawn (we're not flying). Easy walking trails through forest reserves at Roc de Chère.

French: randonnée = hike. refuge = mountain hut you can hike to and eat at.

Easy Food

Markets, bakeries & cooking at the house

Default food mode: bakery in the morning, market food for the house, cheese / fruit / bread always available, and a few real dinners out. Vieux Annecy market runs Tuesday, Friday, Sunday; Saint-Jorioz on Thursday morning.

French: marché = market. boulangerie = bakery. fromagerie = cheese shop.

Tiny glossary

French & lake words, translated.

The cheat sheet for anyone who doesn't spend their free time on French geography or lake vocab.

Lac d'Annecy
The lake. Don't say "Lake Annecy" out loud — locals say lac d'Annecy.
Plage
Beach. Often grass or pebbles around the lake. Saint-Jorioz is the only true sand.
Ponton
Pontoon / floating dock. Some are public (Menthon, Talloires), some are private — scout before swimming up.
Voie verte
Greenway. The lakeside bike/pedestrian path separated from cars.
Marché
Market. Mornings only. Bring a tote, leave with cheese and apricots.
Boulangerie
Bakery. The morning move. Croissant, baguette, pain au chocolat.
Vieille ville
Old town. In Annecy: canals, arcades, markets, restaurants.
Petit lac
The narrower, deeper south end of the lake — Duingt to Bout-du-Lac.
Pavillon Bleu
"Blue Flag" — the certified-clean-water beaches. Saint-Jorioz and Veyrier carry it.
