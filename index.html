Food

What to actually eat.

Markets

Vieux Annecy.

Sunday, Tuesday, Friday. Tomatoes, peaches, charcuterie, three cheeses you didn’t mean to buy. Most meals start here.

Apéro

By the water.

Late-afternoon at Angon or Menthon. Saucisson, Reblochon, bread, tomato. Bridges the swim and dinner.

Cheese

Reblochon, Tomme, Beaufort.

Local. Buy direct from the Aravis farms if we go up — raw, much better.

Fish

Féra & perch.

If a menu has féra or filets de perche, order that.

Beach lunch

Feet near the water.

Poisson Rouge in Sévrier. L’Aquarama at Angon. Eat ten metres from where you’ll swim again.

Savoyard

One cheesy night.

Tartiflette, raclette or fondue once — not three times in August. Save it for the cooler evening, ideally up the hill.

Baguette, pain de campagne, brioche, croissants. The Sévrier bakery is enough.

Notes

Logistics.

Markets

Annecy old town: Sun (big), Tue, Fri — mornings. Sévrier: weekly, smaller.

August: small places close 1–2 weeks. Check the day before.

Beaches

Most public beaches are free; chairs, parasols, paddling pools paid (Talloires, Saint-Jorioz, Menthon). La Brune at Veyrier is free Pavillon Bleu.

Lifeguards: July–August on the bigger municipal beaches. Otherwise swim at your own risk.

Bikes / Voie Verte

Annecy → Sévrier → Saint-Jorioz → Duingt → Doussard. Separated path most of the way.

Rental in Annecy and Sévrier. Book ahead in August.

Boats, SUP, kayaks

SUP and pedal boats at most bigger beaches. Electric boats (no licence) from Annecy and Sévrier.

Getting around

Compagnie des Bateaux du Lac d’Annecy: lake-bus boats between most towns in summer. Useful for bike-one-way days.

East-shore road jams in the afternoon. Ride or boat when you can.

Heights

Skipping paragliding launches, via-ferrata, cliff jumps. We watch from the lawn or the trail — better seat anyway.

Rope swings: scout the depth and entry in person.

French words

Plage — beach. Buvette — beach snack bar. Ponton — pontoon. Voie Verte — separated bike path. Apéro — drinks + snacks before dinner. Marché — market. Reblochon — the cheese. Féra / perche — lake fish.
