The Lake

Lac d'Annecy is shaped like a bent finger pointing south: the wider Grand Lac in the north (Annecy at the tip), the narrower Petit Lac curving southwest, the pinch point at Duingt where a castle sits on a tiny peninsula. Our house is the green star on the west shore.

East Shore vs. West Shore

The eternal debate. Here's the honest breakdown.

East Shore

Morning Sun
Yes. Gorgeous sunrise on the water.
Vibe
Quieter, more upscale, slightly "French countryside estate" energy.
Walkability
Low. Narrow roads, need a car for most things.
Bike Path
No Voie Verte. Narrow road with traffic. Not great for casual cycling.
Dining
Fewer but fancier. More sit-down, reservation-type spots.
Adventure
Closer to Talloires, paragliding, canyoning, Roc de Cherè.
Grocery Run
Small shops only. Serious shopping = drive to Annecy.

West Shore

Afternoon Sun
Yes. Golden hour on the water. Shadier mornings.
Vibe
More practical, family-friendly, lived-in. Think "locals actually live here."
Walkability
High in Sévrier/St-Jorioz. Bakeries, restaurants, markets on foot.
Bike Path
Voie Verte runs right through. 42km car-free path. This is the one.
Dining
More casual variety. Crêperies, lakefront cafés, market food.
Adventure
Slightly further from the big-ticket stuff, but bike path is unbeatable.
Grocery Run
Carrefour in Sévrier. You're set.
The honest bottom line: West Shore is the more practical call for 5 people splitting one car. You can walk to shops, bike everywhere, and get to Annecy in 10 minutes. East Shore is prettier in the morning and closer to the adventure hubs, but you'll drive for everything. If a killer East Shore property appears at the right price, take it. Otherwise, West Shore is the safer bet for how this group actually operates.

Town by Town

East Shore

Veyrier-du-Lac

~20 min drive from house · East Shore
"The quiet money town. If Annecy is the star, Veyrier is its private garden."

The closest East Shore town to Annecy. Beautiful residential area with some of the best lake views. Home to upscale villas with private docks. Limited shops but gorgeous setting. The lakefront road between Veyrier and Menthon is one of the most scenic drives in the area.

Morning sun Lake views Private docks Quiet

Menthon-Saint-Bernard

~25 min drive from house · East Shore
"The castle town. Yes, the one that inspired Sleeping Beauty."

Postcard village dominated by the Château de Menthon, which has stood for nearly 1,000 years and reportedly inspired Disney's castle design. Great beach (Plage de Menthon), a handful of restaurants, and access to the Roc de Chère nature reserve. More village-feel than Veyrier.

Castle Morning sun Good beach Roc de Chère access Restaurants

Talloires-Montmin

~25 min drive from house · East Shore
"Adventure HQ. This is where the canyons, the cliffs, and the paragliders live."

Tucked into a bay on the east shore, Talloires is the gateway to serious adventure: Canyon d'Angon, La Tournette summit, paragliding from Col de la Forclaz, and the Roc de Chère trails. New gravel-bike café "Basecamp" just opened lakeside. The bay itself is stunning for kayaking.

Adventure hub Paragliding Canyon d'Angon Basecamp café

West Shore

Sévrier · This is us

~5 min walk from house · West Shore · Home turf
"The practical one. Carrefour, bike path, beaches, bakeries. What more do you want?"

Our village. Walk to Carrefour, multiple bakeries, restaurants, and the beach. The Voie Verte bike path runs straight through and right past the house. Sévrier beach is popular but there are quieter spots along the shore. The most "livable" base on the lake.

Voie Verte Walkable Carrefour Multiple beaches Bakeries

Saint-Jorioz

~10-15 min by bike from house · West Shore
"Sévrier's slightly more laid-back neighbor with the best public beach on the lake."

One town south of Sévrier with a gorgeous sandy public beach (Plage de Saint-Jorioz), a small port, and a village center with shops and restaurants. The Voie Verte runs through here too. Slightly less commercial than Sévrier, slightly more vacation-town feel. Great farmers market.

Voie Verte Best beach Small port Farmers market Village center

Duingt

~25 min by bike from house · West Shore (at the pinch)
"The charming pinch point. Castle on a peninsula. Cycling café. Pump track. Chef's kiss."

Duingt sits where the Grand Lac narrows into the Petit Lac, marked by the photogenic Château de Duingt on its tiny peninsula. Home to Le Bon Wagon (the cycling café), a 1,000m² pump track with three circuits, and a couple of excellent restaurants. Small and quiet, but perfectly positioned between both shores.

Voie Verte Pump track Castle Le Bon Wagon café Petit Lac access

What We're Doing

Two weeks, one lake, zero excuses. Browse by category.

Must-Do
Kayak/SUP to Hidden Coves
2-3 hours · ~€20-30/person rental · Easy
Paddle from any beach toward the Petit Lac (south end) to find hidden coves inaccessible by land. The water is absurdly clear. Bring snacks. Doussard end has the quietest, least-discovered spots.
Sunset SUP + Apéro
2 hours · ~€20-30 rental · Peak vibes
Paddle out around 7pm from the west shore (afternoon sun side), bring a bottle of rosé in a dry bag, anchor up somewhere scenic, watch the mountains turn pink. This is the move.
Hidden Beach Hopping by Kayak
Half day · ~€25 rental · Chill
Rent a kayak or SUP and explore the small coves along the southern end (Bout du Lac area). Doussard has quiet beaches you can only reach by water. Crystal clear, warm in August, zero crowds. Bring a book.
Highly Worthwhile
Beach Day at Plage de Saint-Jorioz
All day · Free · Maximum chill
Best public beach on the lake. Sandy, spacious, clean water. Arrive before 10am in August to claim a spot. Swim, read, nap, repeat.
Pédalos + Lake Picnic
2-3 hours · ~€15-25/hour rental · Peak social
Rent a pédalo in Annecy or Saint-Jorioz, pack charcuterie and cheese, float around the Grand Lac. It's goofy. It's perfect.
Evening Laps at Plage d'Albigny
1 hour · Free · Post-dinner swim
The beach in Annecy proper. After 6pm the day-trippers clear out and the water is golden. Long open-water swim parallel to shore.
Swim-Accessible Waterfall (Cascade d'Angon)
2 hours · Free · Easy-Moderate hike
Short hike to a waterfall that drops into a pool near the lake. Cool off under the falls. More scenic than strenuous. Good morning activity before a beach afternoon.
Must-Do
Voie Verte Loop (42km)
3-4 hours · Free · All levels
The flat, car-free bike path circling the entire lake. Rent bikes and do the full loop. Non-negotiable group activity. Stop for ice cream in Saint-Jorioz, a drink in Duingt, swim in Talloires.
Duingt Pump Track + Le Bon Wagon
1-2 hours · Free (pump track) · All levels
1,000m² pump track with three circuits right by the lake. Then coffee (or something stronger) at Le Bon Wagon, the cycling café. The pump track is genuinely fun for all skill levels.
Highly Worthwhile
Gravel Routes + Basecamp Café
Half to full day · Rental ~€50-80/day · Moderate
Basecamp in Talloires just opened: gravel bike rental, guided tours, café, and repair shop right on the lake. Four mapped gravel routes. Ask about the Col des Aravis loop.
Mountain Bike Parks (La Clusaz / Le Grand-Bornand)
Half day · Lift pass ~€20-35 · Intermediate+
Ski resorts in summer mode with lift-served bike parks. About 40 min drive. Enduro and flow trails. Le Grand-Bornand also has a pump track.
Ice Cream Tour by Bike
2 hours · ~€5/stop · Essential research
Glacier des Alpes in Annecy, the artisan shops in Saint-Jorioz, the random place in Duingt that's been there since forever. Multiple stops required for data integrity.
Must-Do
Tandem Paragliding from Col de la Forclaz
15-25 min flight · ~€100-130 · No experience needed
THE iconic Annecy experience. Launch from 1,150m and soar over the lake. One of Europe's best paragliding sites. Book 1-2 weeks in advance for August. Ask about acrobatic options for the brave.
Canyon d'Angon (Full Descent)
3-4 hours · ~€50-65/person guided · Intermediate-Hard
Rappelling down waterfalls, natural slides, jumps up to 8m. Book with Vertical Aventure or Terreo Canyoning. August = book in advance.
Accrobranche (Treetop Course)
2-3 hours · ~€25-35/person · Talloires or Semnoz
Acro'Aventures in Talloires has courses perched above the lake with zip lines. Good group activity when you want adventure without the full-day commitment.
Highly Worthwhile
La Tournette Summit (2,351m)
6-7 hours · Free · Difficult (chains, scrambling)
The highest peak overlooking the lake. 360° panorama from Mont Blanc to the Chartreuse. 1,394m elevation gain, cables and chains near the top. Start early (6-7am) to beat August crowds. Not for the faint of heart.
Outdoor Rock Climbing
Half day · ~€50-70 guided · All levels
Limestone cliffs around the lake with 80+ sites. Bureau des Guides d'Annecy offers guided sessions on lakeside crags. Multi-pitch routes available for experienced climbers.
Cliff Jumping at Roc de Chère
2 hours · Free · Moderate risk
The nature reserve has cliff faces dropping into crystal-clear deep water. Hike through the forest to find the spots. Not officially sanctioned but very much done.
Via Ferrata
3-4 hours · ~€45-60/person guided · Moderate
Several via ferrata routes in the Annecy Mountains area. Exposed traverses with lake views below. Guided options through Bureau des Guides d'Annecy.
Intense Canyoning (Montmin)
4-5 hours · ~€60-75/person · Hard
More technical than Angon. Bigger drops, longer descent. For when the regular canyon isn't enough.
Easier Options
Bout du Lac Nature Reserve + Beaver Trail
50 min · Free · Very easy (flat, 3.4km)
Flat walking trail through the marshes where the Ire and Eau Morte rivers meet the lake. European beavers were reintroduced here in 1972 and you can see their tree work everywhere. Zero elevation gain.
Roc de Chère Easy Loop
1.5 hours · Free · Easy
Forested nature reserve between Menthon and Talloires. Beautiful viewpoints, swimming access from the rocks, trails mostly to yourself in the morning. A forested hill with incredible views, not a mountain.
Must-Do
Annecy Old Town + Market
Half day · Free (minus the cheese you'll buy) · Tue/Fri/Sun market
Canals, pastel buildings, the Palais de l'Île. Sunday market is huge. Friday is the smaller local one (better vibes). Reblochon from the fromagerie, bread from the boulangerie, and a glass of Roussette on the canal.
Col de la Forclaz Sunset
30 min drive · Free · Go after 7pm
Drive up to the Col de la Forclaz for the most iconic viewpoint over the lake. Watch paragliders launch at golden hour. Bring wine (or Pellegrino). This is the one that'll make everyone understand why this trip exists.
Group Cooking Night with Market Haul
Evening · Budget varies · Communal vibes
Hit the market, buy everything, and go full chef mode. Tartiflette, raclette, or a full Savoyard spread. Pair with local Roussette or Mondeuse wine.
Highly Worthwhile
Château de Menthon Visit
1.5 hours · ~€10/person · The Disney castle
Nearly 1,000-year-old castle overlooking the lake. Reportedly inspired the Sleeping Beauty castle. Guided tours available. Go late afternoon for the best light.
Annecy Old Town Night Out
Evening · Budget varies · Social
The canals, the bars, the gelato. Start with dinner on Rue Sainte-Claire, drinks along the Thiou canal, gelato at Glacier des Alpes.
Waterfront Restaurant Crawl
Evening · €30-60/person · Dress up (a little)
Le Poisson Rouge (wooden terrace literally in the water, local fish), Le Balcon du Lac (fondue with a view), and the restaurants along the Annecy canal. Photo ops guaranteed.
Tahiti Plage by SUP
Half day · Board rental ~€20 · Easy
Paddle to Plage de l'Impérial or find the smaller beaches between Veyrier and Menthon. Pack lunch. Stay for hours.

Chamonix-Mont-Blanc

1h 15min drive

Aiguille du Midi cable car (3,842m) for jaw-dropping Mont Blanc views. Walk the glass Skywalk. Hike to Lac Blanc. Full-day commitment but unforgettable. ~€70 for cable car round trip.

Yvoire

50 min drive

Medieval village on Lake Geneva, officially one of France's "Most Beautiful Villages." Cobbled streets, 700-year-old ramparts, the Jardin des Cinq Sens. Perfect half-day. Combine with Geneva.

🇨🇭

Geneva

45 min drive

Quick international hop for lunch by Lake Geneva, the Jet d'Eau, Old Town, and Swiss chocolate. Pair with Yvoire for a full day. Remember: Switzerland is expensive. Very expensive.

⛏️

La Clusaz

35 min drive

Ski resort in summer mode. Lift-served mountain biking, hiking trails, alpine lake swimming, and excellent Reblochon cheese from the farms that invented it.

🧀

Thônes

25 min drive

Capital of Reblochon cheese. Visit the cooperative, taste the real thing, learn more about cheese than you ever wanted to. Thursday morning market is the local one. Combine with La Clusaz.

🌊

Gorges du Fier

15 min drive

Dramatic narrow canyon with a walkway bolted to the cliff face 25m above a rushing river. Quick visit (1 hour), super impressive. Good rainy-day option.

🏔️

Le Semnoz

20 min drive

The mountain right behind Annecy. Panoramic views of the lake, Mont Blanc, and the Aravis range. Easy summit hike, picnic spots, paragliding launch. Sunset up here is ridiculous.

Where to Eat

🍷 Special Night Out

Le Poisson Rouge
Wooden terrace extending into the lake. Local fish (perch, féra), Savoyard specialties. The one that looks like it's floating on water. ~€35-50/person.
Le Balcon du Lac
Tartiflette, raclette, and fondue with lake views. The full Savoyard experience without pretension. Great for the group. ~€25-40/person.
L'Osmose du Lac
Lakefront dining with a focus on local, seasonal ingredients. Beautiful setting, reasonable prices for the view. ~€30-45/person.
Auberge du Père Bise (Brasserie side)
The brasserie at this Talloires institution is the casual way to enjoy the stunning lakefront setting without the tasting menu commitment. Local dishes, gorgeous terrace. ~€30-50/person.

🥖 Casual / Don't Miss

Annecy Market Lunch
Sunday or Friday market: rotisserie chicken, cheeses, charcuterie, bread, and a bottle of Roussette on the canal. The best meal of the trip will cost €8/person.
Le Bon Wagon (Duingt)
Cycling café by the pump track. Coffee, pastries, and the vibe of a place that gets it. Post-ride essential.
Glacier des Alpes
Ice cream institution in Annecy Old Town. The line is long. The ice cream is worth it. Get the Génépi flavor for the full Alpine experience.
Any Ferme-Auberge in the mountains
Farm restaurants serving what they raise: Reblochon, diots (Savoyard sausages), polenta, and tarte aux myrtilles. Ask locals for their favorite. Usually €15-25 for a huge meal.
Crêperies in Annecy Old Town
Buckwheat galèttes (savory) and crêpes (sweet) are a Breton transplant that thrives here. Cheap, filling, and legitimately delicious. A good rainy-day lunch move. ~€10-15/person.
Boulangerie run (every morning, non-negotiable)
Croissants, pain au chocolat, and a baguette tradition for the house. This is not optional. Whoever wakes up first is on bakery duty. Budget: ~€2/person/day.
The food philosophy: Cook most nights with market ingredients (Liv's on it), eat out 3-4 special nights, and never skip the morning boulangerie run. The best meals in this region are the simple ones: good bread, good cheese, good wine, lake view.
