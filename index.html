Around the lake

Each town is its own little world.

Going clockwise from where we’ll mostly be. Read these like postcards from someone who keeps coming back, not a database.

West shore · home base

Sévrier

Sévrier is the luxury of the trip because it makes the lake casual. Not dramatic. Not precious. Just: wake up, walk down, swim, come back, get on the bike path, do it again later. This is the town that turns the lake into furniture.

Best for
Slow mornings. Repeat swims. Bike-loop launches.
Water move
Walk-in swim from the public stretch; rinse, towel, go again before lunch.
Food move
Bakery breakfast, market run for cheese and tomatoes, dinner at the house.
Best time
Any. Especially the boring afternoon nobody planned.
Nearby
Annecy in 20 min by bike north; Saint-Jorioz 10 min south.

The older sister read: don’t over-plan from here. Sévrier rewards the day where the only commitment is “we’ll see what time the light is good.”

Top of the lake · the city one

Annecy & Vieux Annecy

You go for the market, the canals, the old town, the gelato, the wandering. You do not need a plan. Annecy is usually better if the plan is “walk until someone points at something edible.” Bike in along the lake path so the city earns itself.

Best for
Market mornings. Long aimless walks. Dinner that becomes ice cream.
Water move
Plage des Marquisats for a city swim; Pâquier lawns for sitting still.
Food move
Tuesday/Friday/Sunday market in the old town. Tarts, cheese, charcuterie, peaches.
Best time
Morning for the market, late afternoon for the canals, evening for everything else.
Nearby
Bike home to Sévrier at golden hour; the path is the point.

The older sister read: it’s touristy in the way Venice is touristy — that doesn’t make it less worth it, just don’t try to “do” it. Drift.

West shore · sandy beach

Saint-Jorioz

Not a rocky little towel spot where everyone pretends they’re comfortable. A real sandy beach. Lawn, shade, volleyball, pétanque, a diving board, summer lifeguards — the kind of beach day where the day stretches because nobody’s annoyed.

Best for
The full beach-day setup. Swim, snack, swim again. Kids and adults equally.
Water move
Plage municipale: shallow entry, supervised in summer, diving board for the show-offs.
Food move
Beach snack at the buvette; or pack a picnic from the morning market.
Best time
11–6. Earlier on hot weekends — the lot fills.
Nearby
Duingt 5 min south for lunch; Sévrier 10 min north.

The older sister read: this is the only place on the lake that genuinely does “normal beach day.” Don’t waste it on a 90-minute dip.

Where the lake pinches · castle town

Duingt

Duingt is the bike-to-lunch town. The ride here from Sévrier or Saint-Jorioz is the point — the water gets dramatically clearer south of the narrows, the château sits on its own little promontory, and the town itself is small enough to feel earned.

Best for
Bike-and-eat days. A long lunch on the lake side. Slow swim before riding home.
Water move
Tiny public swim spots either side of the village; clearest water on the lake.
Food move
Lakeside lunch with a view onto the petit lac and Talloires across the way.
Best time
Late morning to early afternoon, so the light is on the château.
Nearby
Doussard / Bout-du-Lac 10 min south by bike.

The older sister read: don’t skip arriving here by bike. Showing up by car kills 80% of why Duingt is good.

Bottom of the lake · grassy & flat

Doussard / Bout-du-Lac

The bottom of the lake. Big grassy beach, shallow approach, mountains parked all around you. It’s the “lay flat in the grass and not move for three hours” kind of beach. Good entry point for SUP and pedal boats — the water is calmer here.

Best for
Hot, lazy days. Big group sprawl. SUP / kayak / pedal boat launches.
Water move
Walk-in entry from the lawns; paddle out toward the reserve and float.
Food move
Picnic over restaurant — the appeal is the lawn, not the menu.
Best time
Afternoon, when the sun crosses to the south end.
Nearby
Voie Verte loops south from here; Forclaz viewpoint is the closer alpine option.

The older sister read: this is the day where someone falls asleep on the towel and nobody minds.

East shore · the postcard one

Talloires

Talloires is the one that sells the lake. Diving boards, slide, paddling pool, a nautic centre, and the view back across the petit lac to Château de Duingt that everybody photographs and nobody captures. The beach is more “summer club” than “wild swim,” but on the right afternoon that’s exactly what you want.

Best for
Showy lake-life day. Diving boards, ping-pong, lunch out, swim, repeat.
Water move
Plage de Talloires-Montmin: full set-up, paddling pool, slide, diving boards.
Food move
Lake-edge lunch in town; this is the dinner-out side of the lake.
Best time
Late afternoon for the light onto Duingt; evening for dinner.
Nearby
Angon 5 min south; Menthon 5 min north.

The older sister read: book dinner if a Saturday. Otherwise just turn up and improvise.

East shore · quiet swim with a view

Angon

Angon is Talloires’ quieter little sibling. A small stream runs into the lake, there’s a bar/restaurant on the beach, beach games, and the same Duingt-château view minus the volume. If Talloires feels too much, walk five minutes south and you’re here.

Best for
Long quiet swim. Beach apéro. Couples / smaller hangs.
Water move
Walk-in from the small public beach; the stream entry keeps it cooler.
Food move
L’Aquarama-style beach restaurant for lunch with feet basically in the lake.
Best time
Late afternoon into apéro hour — the light here is unfair.
Nearby
Cascade d’Angon walk-up, very short, very mossy, very cool.

The older sister read: this is the “we accidentally stayed for dinner” beach.

East shore · pontoon energy

Menthon-Saint-Bernard

Menthon is polished lake energy: fixed and floating pontoons, diving boards, a lakeside promenade you can wander on after dinner, a snack bar/restaurant, and the cleanest lawns on the lake. Up the hill is Château de Menthon brooding over the whole thing.

Best for
Pontoon swims. Diving-board afternoons. Walk-after-dinner kind of nights.
Water move
Plage de Menthon: walk to the end of the pontoon, jump, repeat.
Food move
Snack bar at the beach; proper dinners up in the village or back toward Talloires.
Best time
Late afternoon, into golden hour on the pontoons.
Nearby
Veyrier-du-Lac 5 min north; Roc de Chère trails inland.

The older sister read: pontoon day is non-negotiable. This is the one to do it on.

East shore · clean swim, no fuss

Veyrier-du-Lac · La Brune

Veyrier is the locals-but-not-secret swim. La Brune is a Pavillon Bleu beach, free and public, with a clean swim and a view straight back across to the west shore. No diving boards, no slide — just a good, honest swim and a flat lawn.

Best for
Quick high-quality swim. Less-busy weekday afternoons.
Water move
La Brune walk-in; clear water, supervised stretches in summer.
Food move
Eat back in Annecy or Talloires — Veyrier’s food story is thin.
Best time
Late morning before the wind kicks up across the petit lac.
Nearby
Menthon 5 min south; Annecy 10 min by bike.

The older sister read: this is the “we just want to swim and go” option. Don’t overthink it.

East shore · nature reserve

Roc de Chère

The forested promontory between Talloires and Menthon. Easy shaded trails, a nature reserve, and clear water below. There are cliff-jumping spots — we are watching them, not jumping off them. Treat it like a quiet woods walk that ends with a swim from a calm pebbly entry.

Best for
A short woods walk that breaks up a swim day. Cooler than open beach.
Water move
Pebbly walk-in entries on the south side. Avoid the high-rock launches.
Food move
Pack a snack; eat afterwards in Talloires or Menthon.
Best time
Mid-afternoon when the open-shore beaches start filling.
Nearby
Loops back into Talloires or Menthon on foot.

The older sister read: this is a scout-first place. Walk it, look at it, get in only where the entry is friendly.

What kind of day?

Pick the mood. The day picks itself.

Not itineraries. The shape of a day, in story form. Tap a mood to spotlight the days that fit; the towns inside link out.

Lazy lake day

The day that does itself.

Wake up in Sévrier. Walk to the water. Swim. Bakery. Lawn. Swim again. Long afternoon at Saint-Jorioz or the southern grass at Doussard. Dinner is whatever’s in the fridge. Nobody got in a car.

Sévrier→Saint-Jorioz→Doussard→home

Bike-to-swim

The reason the bike path exists.

Voie Verte south from Sévrier. Stop in Duingt for lunch on the lake. Push to Bout-du-Lac if legs allow, or just turn around. The water under the bridges south of Duingt is the clearest you’ll see all trip.

Sévrier→Saint-Jorioz→Duingt lunch→Bout-du-Lac

Pontoon & clear water

The day that earns the photos.

Cross to the east shore. Menthon for the floating pontoons and diving boards. Walk south through Roc de Chère woods. Land in Angon for a quiet second swim and apéro by the stream.

Menthon pontoons→Roc de Chère walk→Angon swim

Market to dinner

One ingredient at a time.

Bike into Annecy for the morning market. Cheese, charcuterie, peaches, a tart for later. Swim at Marquisats, drift through the canals, sit at the Pâquier. Ride home golden hour. Cook everything you bought.

Annecy market→old town wander→Pâquier→home dinner

Lake-side dinner

The night the lake earns it.

Late swim at Angon or Menthon. Rinse. Eat at Talloires with feet basically in the water. Walk the promenade afterwards. Drive home in the dark, windows down. (Easily Andrew-friendly — no drinking-centric.)

Angon swim→Talloires dinner→lake walk

Big view, feet on earth

Mountains without flinging anyone off.

Drive up to the Semnoz ridge for a picnic and the whole-lake view. Or watch the paragliders launch from Forclaz from the ground (it’s the better seat anyway), and have lunch at the farm. We like mountains. We don’t need to fall off them.

Semnoz picnic·or Forclaz from below

Worth-leaving day

Only if it’s actually worth it.

Not every day off-lake earns itself. Aravis cheese loop almost always does. Chamonix on a clear day, yes. Geneva, only if someone really wants to. Yvoire and Gorges du Fier are half-day add-ons, not full days.

Food moves

Eat where the lake actually eats.

Not a restaurant database. The handful of food rituals that make the trip feel like the trip — apéro at the water, cheese damage, lake dinners, and the markets that feed the rest of it.

Market mornings

The market is the trip.

Vieux Annecy on Sunday, Tuesday and Friday. Tomatoes, peaches, charcuterie, three cheeses you didn’t mean to buy, a tart for later. Most lunches and dinners on this trip start as a market run.

Apéro by the water

The most useful meal of the day isn’t a meal.

Late-afternoon apéro at Angon or Menthon — saucisson, Reblochon, olives, tomato, bread, lemonade or pastis or fizzy water, doesn’t matter. The swim happens before, the dinner happens later, this is the bit that ties them together.

Cheese damage

Reblochon & friends.

The cheeses you actually want here are local: Reblochon (raw, runny, smelly, perfect), Tomme de Savoie, Beaufort. Buy from the Aravis farms if we do that day trip — direct, raw, much better.

Lake fish

Féra & perch.

If a menu has féra du lac or filets de perche from the lake, get those. They’re the local thing, not steak.

Beach lunch

Feet-in-water restaurants.

Poisson Rouge in Sévrier; L’Aquarama / similar at Angon. The point isn’t the food — it’s eating it ten metres from where you’ll swim back in.

Savoyard dinner

The one cheesy night.

Tartiflette, raclette or fondue once on the trip — not three times, that’s a tourist mistake in 30°C August. Do it the one cooler evening, ideally up the hill (Talloires village, or the Aravis if we’re there).

Bakery

The boring miracle.

Baguette, pain de campagne, brioche, croissants, a quiche or two. Repeat. The bakery in Sévrier is enough for the whole trip.

Big views, feet on earth

Mountains without the height panic.

The point of these is not the summit. The point is being up high enough that the lake makes sense — and being on a wide trail or a road or a lawn while it happens. We like mountains. We do not need to fling ourselves off them.

~1700 m · road access

Semnoz ridge picnic

Drive up. Walk a flat-ish ridge. Whole-lake view, Mont Blanc on a clear day. Bring a picnic from the market. The drive itself is half the experience.

Ground-level, no edge exposure. The road does the climbing.

~1250 m · road + lawn

Col de la Forclaz, watched from below

The big paragliding launch. We are watching, not jumping. Lunch at La Ferme de la Forclaz on the lawn while the wings drop toward the lake — better seat than launching anyway.

Stay on the lawn / restaurant side. Easy to enjoy without going near the edge.

Loop · easy

Circuit des 3 Croix

A short loop above Annecy with three crosses and the same lake-view payoff without the drive up Semnoz. Wide trails, a few clearings, no scrambling.

Forest path. Open viewpoints, no scrambles.

Viewpoint · drive-up

Taillefer / Crêt de Châtillon

If we’re already on Semnoz and want one more lookout. Park, walk a few minutes, see the lake from a slightly different angle.

Wide path, lawn-style summit.

~1830 m · cave plateau

Parmelan plateau (caveat)

Limestone plateau, dramatic in photos. The famous via-cable route to the top is an exposure problem for us — skip it. The plateau itself, accessed gently, is fine for a careful walk.

Stick to the gentle approach. Skip Pas de l’Aulp / cabled sections.

River canyon · easy

Gorges du Fier

Walkway pinned along a narrow river canyon. Walls are close, water is loud, it’s in shade. Kids- and adults-equally fun, low effort, ~30 min.

Boardwalk has rails. Low/no exposure feel for most people.

If we leave the lake

Ranked by whether it’s worth the drive.

Not all day trips are equal. These are the ones siblings ask about, ranked honestly. Lake days are the trip — these are the days the lake gives you off.

01

Aravis & La Clusaz · Reblochon farms

Closer than Chamonix, prettier than people think, and the cheese is from across the road. A couple of farms sell raw Reblochon directly. Loop down through Thônes on the way home.

Worth it
02

Chamonix & Mont Blanc valley

The big one. Worth it on a clear day, even just to walk Chamonix and stare at the Aiguilles. Skip the Aiguille du Midi cable car — exposure problem, also a queue. Train up the valley to Montenvers + walk is plenty.

Worth it
03

Gorges du Fier (½ day)

Already in views. Do it as a half-day pairing with lunch in Annecy or Sévrier on the way back. Don’t make it the whole trip.

Half-day
04

Yvoire on Lac Léman

Stone village on Lake Geneva, very pretty, very known. A nice afternoon if we want to swap lakes for a day; not the day to expect peace and quiet in August.

Maybe
05

Megève

Posh ski town in summer mode. Pretty walk-around, good food, expensive coffee. Pleasant if we’re passing on the way to/from Chamonix; not a destination day on its own.

Pair with Chamonix
06

Geneva

Border city, jet d’eau, Old Town, currency change, cleaner streets, prices that aren’t. Worth half a day if a sibling is curious; otherwise the lake is winning.

Only if curious
07

Gruyères (Switzerland)

Cheesy in two ways. A long drive for what’s basically a fortified village + cheese factory + Giger museum. Charming, but two hours each way during a lake summer is a lot.

Skip this trip
Useful, boring things

The small stuff, kept small.

Market days & opening rhythm

Annecy old town: Sunday (the big one), Tuesday, Friday — mornings. Sévrier: smaller, weekly. Saint-Jorioz, Talloires: weekly summer markets.

August reality: many small businesses close 1–2 weeks for vacation. If a specific bakery/restaurant matters, check the day before.

Beaches: free, paid, lifeguard

Most public beaches around the lake are free with paid options for chairs, parasols, paddling pool, etc. (Talloires, Saint-Jorioz, Menthon). Veyrier’s La Brune is free Pavillon Bleu.

Lifeguards generally July–August on the bigger municipal beaches; outside those hours/months it’s swim-at-your-own-risk.

Bikes & the Voie Verte

Voie Verte runs from Annecy down the west shore through Sévrier, Saint-Jorioz, Duingt and into Doussard — fully separated bike path most of the way, one of the best in Europe.

Bike rental in Annecy and Sévrier (regular, electric, kids’ bikes). Book ahead in peak August.

Boats, SUP, kayaks

SUP and pedal-boat rental at almost every bigger beach. Electric boat rental (no licence needed, low speeds) from Annecy and Sévrier — fun for a circling lunch loop.

Getting around

Lake bus + boat (Compagnie des Bateaux du Lac d’Annecy) connects most lakeside towns in summer. Useful for one-way bike-then-boat days.

Driving: the east-shore road gets clogged on summer afternoons; ride or boat instead when you can.

Heights / safety scout note

Group is height-averse. We’re skipping paragliding launches, via-ferrata, and cliff-jumping spots as activities. We can still watch them happen — Forclaz launch from the lawn, Roc de Chère cliffs from the trail — and that’s usually the better seat.

Rope swings: people mention them on this lake. We’re not naming exact spots — scout in person, look at the depth and the entry, only commit if obviously safe.

French / lake words

Plage — beach. Buvette — beach snack bar. Ponton — pontoon. Voie Verte — separated bike path. Apéro — drinks + small food before dinner; the most important meal that isn’t one. Marché — market. Reblochon — the cheese; soft, raw, Aravis. Féra / perche — lake fish on menus.
