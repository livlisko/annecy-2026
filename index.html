Where things are

Lake Annecy at a glance.

Annecy at the north. We're staying in Sévrier (west shore). The eastern shore is the dramatic side — good for photos and ride scenery. Buses link both shores from town.

Home base

Sévrier

Practical west-shore village, 10 minutes from old Annecy by bus or bike path. Easy lake access, no logistics overhead, and the start of the loop ride is literally outside.

What this means for the day: walk to the water, ride to old town, swim on the way back. Repeat.

Two shores, in human terms

West shore Sévrier, Saint-Jorioz, Duingt. Flatter, easier by bike, beachy. This is most of our days.
East shore Veyrier, Menthon, Talloires. Mountains rise straight out of the water — beautiful for photos and the second half of the ride.
Buses Line 50A serves the west (Annecy ↔ Sévrier ↔ Saint-Jorioz ↔ Duingt). Line 60 serves the east (Annecy ↔ Veyrier ↔ Menthon ↔ Talloires).
The lake towns

Where to stop, in human terms.

You don't need to memorize any of this. These are just the names you'll hear, with one sentence on what they actually are.

North end Old town Easy default

Annecy & Vieux Annecy

The anchor. Canals, old stone streets, markets, lakefront walks, gelato, restaurants. Every traveler here eventually has an "oh, this is the place" moment in the old town.

French to know: Vieux Annecy / vieille ville = old Annecy / old town. Where we go when we want beauty without logistics.

West shore Home base

Sévrier

Where we sleep. Quiet, lake-adjacent, on the bike path. The loop ride starts outside the door.

West shore Beach day

Saint-Jorioz

The big practical beach: roomy swimming area, shade, facilities, easy on the bike path. The lowest-friction group day on the lake.

West shore Photo stop

Duingt

The little village on the bend with the château-on-the-water silhouette. One of the best "wait, this place is unreal" moments on the loop.

East shore Castle energy

Menthon-Saint-Bernard

Storybook one: shaded beach, fairytale castle on the hill above town. Easy "we swam, then saw something historic" half-day.

East shore Magic beaches

Talloires & Angon coves

The dreamy bay with the postcard views and hidden coves on the road south. Worth a half-day swim trip from Sévrier.

Easy Water Daily default

Lake swims & beach afternoons

The baseline activity: walk or bike to the lake, swim, read, snack, repeat. Lake Annecy is unusually clear and very swim-friendly all summer.

French: plage = beach. May be grass, pebbles, or sand — not always ocean-style.

Best on jet-lag day, recovery days, "we did too much yesterday" days, and most other days.

The lake loop by bike

The "everyone should do it once" ride: 40 km, mostly-protected bike path, with swim stops and snack options the whole way. Photo above is from the actual loop with Dad.

French: voie verte = greenway, a bike/pedestrian path separated from cars.

For non-cyclists this is rented e-bike territory. For cyclists, it's the warm-up.

Medium → spicy Bike

Climb a col (only if you want to)

A col is a mountain pass — a road that climbs up between peaks. In cycling terms, this is where Tour de France drama happens.

Plain English: "Doing a col" = ride uphill for a long time, enjoy a ridiculous view, then descend.

We can pick the level: a gentle scenic climb, a Forclaz day, or a full "why are we like this" alpine route.

Medium Mountain Height-safe

Mountain views, from the ground

The big-picture mountain experience without ridges, chains, or exposure. Forclaz pass for a cinematic view down the lake. La Tournette refuge approach if anyone wants a real walking day.

French: randonnée = hike. refuge = mountain hut you can hike to and eat at.

We watch paragliders launch from the ground at Forclaz — we're not flying.

Tiny glossary

French & mountain words, translated.

The cheat sheet for anyone who doesn't spend their free time on French geography or cycling vocab.

Lac
Lake. Lac d'Annecy = Lake Annecy.
Plage
Beach. Often grass or pebbles around the lake — not always sand.
Voie verte
Greenway. The lakeside bike/pedestrian path separated from cars.
Col
A mountain pass. Cyclists use it to mean "long climb up to a saddle."
Randonnée
Hike. Difficulty varies wildly — bring real shoes either way.
Refuge
Mountain hut. Often the goal of a hike — you can eat there.
Vieille ville
Old town. In Annecy: canals, arcades, markets, restaurants.
Marché
Market. Mornings only. Bring a tote, leave with cheese and apricots.
Boulangerie
Bakery. The morning move. Croissant, baguette, pain au chocolat.
